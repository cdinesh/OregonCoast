Oregon Coast & Inland Adventure: 5-Day Itinerary

Overview: Designed to maximize daylight and scenic routes. Key locations include beaches, viewpoints, lighthouses, waterfalls, and iconic coastal highways.
Sunset ETA Goal: Arrive at each lodging before ~8:45 PM

Day 1 (July 4) – Tillamook

Lodging: The Tillamook Inn, 2515 Main Ave N, Tillamook, OR 97141
Confirmation Number: H7H171WDL
Key Stops & Activities:
  • Cannon Beach: An iconic Oregon Coast classic. You can see Haystack Rock here and it's a popular spot for picnics or bonfires on the beach if the weather permits. While it can be a "gray cold Misty experience," catching nicer weather makes it enjoyable.
  • Neahkahnie Viewpoint: A suggested stop along the Northern Coast. Consider the Neahkahnie Trail hike (1.5 hours) from this area.
  • Tillamook Creamery: A prolific farmer-owned co-op known for making some of the nation's best cheese and ice cream. Upon arrival, you're given a free sample of cheese. You can take a self-guided tour to see how 1.6 million pounds of fresh dairy milk are turned into cheese and ice cream daily, including the "cheddar master" machine.
  • Three Capes Scenic Loop: A recommended scenic drive starting from Tillamook.
  • Cape Kiwanda: This location is home to Pelican Brewing, which has a waterfront location. The fishing fleet of dories here uniquely drives their boats ashore, lifting their motors at the last second, and then trucks use trailers to remove them between waves. You can also climb Cape Kiwanda for grand views of the sandy cliffs and Pacific Ocean. Pacific City, where Cape Kiwanda is located, has no open container law, allowing you to enjoy beers from Pelican Brewing on the beach.
Driving Details:
  • Distance: 95 miles
  • Drive Time: 2 hours 30 minutes
  • Arrive at lodging before sunset (~8:45 PM)
Dining Suggestions:
  • Lunch: Wayfarer Restaurant.
  • Dinner: Pelican Brewing. At Tillamook Creamery, their dining hall offers fresh cheese items like fried cheese curds, grilled cheese, and mac and cheese, topped off with their super creamy ice cream (the "Monster Cookie" flavor is highly recommended).
Sunset Viewpoint: Cape Kiwanda

Day 2 (July 5) – Florence

Lodging: Driftwood Shores Resort, 88416 1st Ave, Florence, OR 97439
Confirmation #: EXP-2210820413
Key Stops & Activities:
  • Devil's Punchbowl (Newport): Explore at low tide. High tide offers dramatic views. Good surfing and tide pool area.
  • Yaquina Head Lighthouse & Tide Pools: Tallest lighthouse on Oregon Coast. Great tide pools. Often seals nearby.
  • Cape Perpetua: Amazing coastal mountain park.
    • Devil's Churn: Most dramatic at high tide. Short hike to viewing ledge.
    • Thor's Well & Spouting Horn: Natural geysers and collapsed sea cave. Best at high tide. Watch for waves!
  • Heceta Head Lighthouse: 0.5-mile scenic hike to lighthouse. Hobbit Trail (4-mile RT) through magical forest to beach.
Driving Details:
Distance: 100 miles
Drive Time: 3 hours 30 minutes
Arrive by sunset (~8:45 PM)
Dining Suggestions:
  • Lunch: Local Ocean Seafoods – Upscale, local-sourced seafood
  • Dinner: Waterfront Depot – Housed in a 1912 train station with fine dining
Sunset Viewpoint: Heceta Head Lighthouse

Day 3 (July 6) – Chemult

Lodging: Eagle Crater Lake Inn, 108915 U.S. 97, Chemult, OR 97731
Confirmation #: 2210823968
Key Stops & Activities:
  • Coquille Point (Bandon): Scenic beach and fun recycled art sculptures
  • Port Orford: Local favorite for fish & chips
  • Samuel H. Boardman Corridor: 12-mile coastal park with iconic viewpoints
    • Natural Bridges: Wooden viewpoint. Do not attempt to cross.
    • Secret Beach: Hidden trail to dreamlike sea stacks and cliffs. Great at sunrise/sunset. 1.5-hour hike.
Driving Details:
Distance: 160 miles
Drive Time: 4 hours 30 minutes
Arrive by sunset (~8:45 PM)
Dining Suggestions:
  • Lunch: Bandon Fish Market
  • Snack: Crazy Norwegian's
Sunset Viewpoint: Secret Beach

Day 4 (July 7) – Bend

Lodging: Holiday Inn Express, 61070 S HWY 97, Bend, OR 97701
Confirmation #: 62279082
Key Stops & Activities:
  • Crater Lake National Park: Spectacular deep blue lake
    • Rim Drive: 33-mile loop with multiple pullouts
    • Cleetwood Cove Trail: 1.5-hour hike down to lake and boat tour access
    • Crater Lake Lodge: Ideal for brunch with a view
  • Optional: Fort Rock geological formation (detour)
Driving Details:
Distance: 135 miles
Drive Time: 3 hours 15 minutes
Arrive by sunset (~8:45 PM)
Dining Suggestions:
  • Brunch: Crater Lake Lodge
  • Dinner: Deschutes Brewery – Local favorite for craft beer and food
Sunset Viewpoint: Crater Lake Rim Drive

Day 5 (July 8) – Portland

Lodging: Kimpton RiverPlace Hotel, 1510 S Harbor Way, Portland, OR
Confirmation #: 29291103
Key Stops & Activities:
  • Mount Hood: Towering peak near Portland
    • Trillium Lake: Great reflection photos of the mountain
    • Timberline Lodge Chairlift: Scenic lift ride (optional)
  • Multnomah Falls: 620-ft 2-tier waterfall. Arrive ~4:30 PM. Hike to top is ~1.5 hours round-trip
  • Historic Columbia River Highway: Gorgeous drive back into Portland
Driving Details:
Distance: 180 miles
Drive Time: 4 hours 15 minutes
Arrive by sunset (~8:45 PM)
Dining Suggestions:
  • Lunch: Timberline Lodge
  • Dinner: Departure (Portland) – High-end modern Asian fusion
Sunset Viewpoint: Crown Point Vista
